Project: Quant Research

skewness-cross-sectional-signal

Python, Pandas, NumPy, SciPy, arch (GARCH), Fama-MacBeth regression
Is stock return skewness a fundamental feature, or just volatility clustering in disguise? A model-independent test of US equity non-Gaussianity, motivated by a tree-level vs. loop-generated analogy from quantum field theory.
Hover to see more →
Quick Summary
Built a 20-year, survivorship-bias-corrected return panel and tested raw skewness and coskewness as cross-sectional signals — both significant in amplitude but unpriced. Fit per-stock GARCH(1,1)-Gaussian null models and used Monte Carlo simulation to show volatility clustering explains under 0.1% of observed skewness, isolating a residual that is significant in every regime tested and traces a structural sign reversal at the 2011 Eurozone crisis.
