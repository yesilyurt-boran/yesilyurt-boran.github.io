Research

My work focuses on quantum gravity, early universe cosmology, and the intersection of high-energy particle physics with cosmological observations. Below are my publications and preprints. Hover over any publication to see a brief summary.

Peer-Reviewed Publications
2025 Journal of Physics A Published

The other ADM

R. P. Woodard, B. Yesilyurt
A study on nonperturbative gravitational UV self-screening, developing a cosmological analogue of the ADM framework and initiating its implementation in coupled gravity–QED systems to explore dynamical regulation of ultraviolet divergences.
Paper arXiv
2025 Classical and Quantum Gravity Published

The third structure function

S. P. Miao, N. C. Tsamis, R. P. Woodard, B. Yesilyurt
Re-derived the graviton self-energy induced by massless minimally coupled scalar loops on de Sitter spacetime, extending the tensor decomposition to cosmological backgrounds by identifying an additional independent contribution beyond the standard Ricci and Weyl structures.
Paper arXiv
2024 Physics Letters B Published

The need to renormalize the cosmological constant

N. C. Tsamis, R. P. Woodard, B. Yesilyurt
Analyzed one-loop graviton self-energy corrections from massless minimally coupled scalars in de Sitter spacetime, showing the necessity of finite cosmological constant renormalization for maintaining conservation and consistency of effective gravitational dynamics.
Paper arXiv
2023 Physical Review D Published

Large inflationary logarithms in a nontrivial nonlinear sigma model

C. Litos, R. P. Woodard, B. Yesilyurt
Computed one- and two-loop infrared logarithmic corrections in nonlinear sigma models with nontrivial field-space curvature, deriving the background evolution and renormalization group functions relevant to inflationary graviton dynamics in de Sitter spacetime.
Paper arXiv
2023 Journal of High Energy Physics Published

Remembrance of things past

R. P. Woodard, B. Yesilyurt
Studied the nonperturbative evolution of infrared effects in nonlinear sigma models on cosmological backgrounds beyond de Sitter space, showing how large-scale correlations generated during primordial inflation can persist and influence late-time cosmological evolution.
Paper arXiv
2023 Journal of High Energy Physics Published

Unfinished business in a nonlinear sigma model on de Sitter background

R. P. Woodard, B. Yesilyurt
Investigated infrared effects in nonlinear sigma models on de Sitter spacetime, completing the two-loop background evolution and one-loop beta function analysis within a stochastic and renormalization group framework for quantum fields in curved spacetime.
